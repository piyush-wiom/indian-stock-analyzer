🏦 Mutual Funds & SIP Guide
Everything about mutual funds and SIP — explained simply for beginners.
💡 What is a Mutual Fund? ▼

Simple explanation: Imagine you and 999 others each put ₹1,000 per month into a common pool. An expert Fund Manager invests that pool in shares, bonds, and other assets. Profits are shared proportionally — that's a mutual fund!


Key Benefits:

  • 🎯 Professional management — experts invest on your behalf
  • 💰 Start small — from ₹100/month
  • 📊 Diversification — one fund holds 50–100 stocks
  • 🛡️ SEBI regulated — government oversight
  • 💧 Liquidity — redeem anytime

NAV (Net Asset Value): The per-unit price of a fund — like a share price for the fund itself.

📅 What is SIP? + SIP Calculator ▼

SIP = Investing a fixed amount every month automatically.

Just like a Netflix auto-payment, SIP money gets invested automatically each month.

Rupee Cost Averaging: When the market is down you buy more units; when it's up you buy fewer. Your average cost stays lower over time.

🚀 How to Get Started ▼
1
Complete KYC — Online KYC using Aadhaar + PAN card. Takes 10–15 minutes on any fund platform.
2
Choose a platform — Zerodha Coin (low cost), Groww (beginner friendly), Paytm Money (easy), or your bank's platform.
3
Pick a fund category — Match your goal and risk appetite. Beginner? Start with an Index Fund.
4
Set your SIP amount — Whatever you're comfortable with. Even ₹500/month works. Increase it later.
5
Invest and stay patient — Don't panic at market ups and downs. Markets always grow over the long term.

📚 How It Works
Understand each technical indicator in plain English.
📊 RSI — Relative Strength Index ▼

What is RSI? — A "speedometer" that measures how fast buying or selling is happening.

Analogy: Like a car going too fast (RSI > 70) — it brakes. Too slow (RSI < 30) — it accelerates.

RSI ValueMeaningScore
< 30Oversold — too many sellers+2 (Strong Buy)
30 – 45Weak zone+1 (Buy)
45 – 55Neutral0 (Hold)
55 – 70Strong zone-1 (Caution)
> 70Overbought — too many buyers-2 (Strong Sell)
📈 MACD — Moving Average Convergence Divergence▼

What is MACD? — The difference between two moving averages (12-day and 26-day). A momentum indicator.

Analogy: Two cars racing. When the fast car (MACD) overtakes the slow car (Signal line) — bullish signal!

SignalMeaningScore
MACD > SignalBullish crossover — upward momentum+1
MACD < SignalBearish crossover — downward momentum-1
📉 Moving Averages — Golden Cross & Death Cross▼

SMA50 = Average price over the last 50 days (short-term trend)

SMA200 = Average price over the last 200 days (long-term trend)

Cross TypeConditionMeaningScore
🌟 Golden CrossSMA50 > SMA200Long-term positive signal+1
💀 Death CrossSMA50 < SMA200Long-term negative signal-1
🎯 Bollinger Bands▼

Bollinger Bands show the normal price range. Three lines: Upper, Middle (SMA20), Lower.

Analogy: Like a rubber band — the further it stretches, the harder it snaps back.

Price PositionMeaningScore
Below lower bandOversold — bounce likely+1
Between bandsNormal range0
Above upper bandOverbought — pullback likely-1
📦 Volume Analysis▼

Volume = number of shares bought/sold on a given day.

Rule: If the price rises and volume is high → the signal is strong. Volume only confirms — it never penalizes.

ConditionScore
5-day avg > 20-day avg × 1.2 (volume spike)+0.5
Normal volume0
🧮 Total Score → Final Recommendation▼

Final Score = RSI + MACD + MA Cross + Bollinger + Volume

Maximum: +5.5 | Minimum: -5.0 | Confidence = |Score| ÷ 5.5 × 100%

ScoreRecommendation
≥ 3.0🚀 Strong Buy
1.0 – 2.9✅ Buy
-1.0 – 0.9⏸️ Hold
-3.0 – -1.1⚠️ Sell
< -3.0🔴 Strong Sell
